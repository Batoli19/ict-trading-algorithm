HOW TO READ THE BOT RESULTS
===========================

Whenever testing or checking the bot's performance, you will see a bunch of numbers. Here is what the most important ones mean.

PROFIT FACTOR (PF):
This is the most critical metric. It tells you how much money the bot makes for every dollar it loses.
- A PF of 1.0 means it broke even (made $1 for every $1 lost).
- A PF of 1.5 means the bot made $1.50 for every $1 it lost.
Anything above 1.2 is good, and above 1.5 is excellent.

WIN RATE:
This is simply the percentage of trades that were profitable. 
You might see a win rate like 64% and think "that doesn't sound that high," but a 64% win rate is actually fantastic. Because the bot usually makes more money on its winning trades than it loses on its losing trades, a 64% or even a 50% win rate can be highly profitable!

PIPS TO DOLLARS:
"Pips" are the points of movement in the market. Depending on your lot size, pips translate to dollars. For a standard trade risking 1% on a standard lot, you can often estimate that roughly 10 pips = $100, though this varies by currency pair and risk parameters. The bot calculates its performance in pips so it scales independently of the account size.

MONTHLY TABLES:
In the backtest CSVs, you will see breakdowns of the bot's performance month-by-month. This is to ensure the bot is consistent. A good strategy will be profitable in most months, rather than making all its money in one lucky month and losing in the rest.

GIVEBACK GUARD ('GIVEBACK_GUARD' EXIT REASON):
Sometimes you'll see a trade was closed due to a "GIVEBACK_GUARD". What this means is the trade was heavily in profit, but the market suddenly reversed. Rather than letting a huge winning trade turn into a full loss, the Giveback Guard stepped in and closed the trade automatically to protect the remaining profit. It's a sign the bot is protecting your capital intelligently.
